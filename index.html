DocNote

DocNote is a study tool geared towards students in medical school who are studying radiology. Its inspiration is the result of multiple months of user research, usability testing and data-driven design. Along with two engineers and a designer, I researched the interactions and behaviors of neuroradiology students at Tufts Medical Center. This involved contextual observation, interviewing, task analysis, & workflow analysis. Our research revealed that students and faculty members participate in critically important conversations when looking over MRI scans; however, students had no efficient, simply, & reliable way to keep track of information for these dialogues. Multiple rounds of prototyping and usability testing lead to a final wireframe design and interaction architecture for DocNote.

EventFindr

EventFindr connects people to events in their area based on their likes & dislikes. Based on a set of user-defined "likes" & "dislikes", EventFindr finds the perfect way to spend the day. EventFindr was prototyped using HTML from a mobile-first perspective. The creation and optimization of the EventFindr prototype invovled comprehensively defining the syntactic & semantic design specifications as well as multiple rounds of usability testing.
